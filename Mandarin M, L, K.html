M

Mingtian = tommorrow

Mandarin: Mingtian tianqi zenmeyang

English: What is the weather tommorrow




Mingzi = name

Mandarin: Ni jiao shenme mingzi

English:  What is your name




mifan = rice

Mandarin: Wo xihuan mifan

English: I like rice




meiyou = there is not
 
Mandarin: Wo mei you shi jian liu zai zeli

English: I don't have time to stay here



Mei guan xi = that's OKAY, don't mention it

Mandarin: [p1] Duibuqi [p2] Mei guanxi

English: [p1] Sorry [p2] Mei guanxi



mao = cat

Mandarin: Wo xihuan mao

English: I like cats



Mai  = purchase

Mandarin: Mai yi song yi

English: Buy one get one free



Ma  = question word(used at end of sentence)

Mandarin: Ni chi le ma?

English: Did you eat



mama = mother

Mandarin: Wo de mama san shi si sui

English: My mom is 34

Did you know?

In the early days of mandarin language.
 Sentences were in opposite order.
For example Ni jiao shen me is what is your name,
 but when you put it word by word, it is "Your name is what?"



L

Liu = six

Mandarin: Wo de mimi liu sui

English: My little sister is six years old




li = interior

Mandarin: Ta kuaisi de zou jin le xiao nongle

English: He walked inside a small alley
